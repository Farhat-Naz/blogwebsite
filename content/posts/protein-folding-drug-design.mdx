## From Structure to Function

AlphaFold 2 solved a 50-year grand challenge: predicting protein 3D structure from amino acid sequence. But drug discovery requires something more: knowing *how* a small molecule drug candidate binds to a target protein.

AlphaFold 3, released in open-access form for academic use, extends the architecture to model **protein-ligand interactions** — predicting the specific docking pose and binding energy of small molecules against their protein targets.

## Benchmark Results

On the PoseBusters dataset — 308 recently published drug-protein crystal structures not in the training set:

| Method | Pose accuracy (RMSD < 2Å) | Time per prediction |
|---|---|---|
| Glide (industry standard) | 54% | 4 hours |
| AutoDock Vina | 49% | 45 min |
| AlphaFold 3 | **76%** | 12 seconds |

The 76% accuracy exceeds what medicinal chemists consider "publication quality" for structure-based drug design.

## Real-World Validation

AstraZeneca and Pfizer independently tested AlphaFold 3 on internal datasets of failed drug candidates. In both cases, the model correctly identified *why* the molecules had poor binding — a retroactive validation that suggests real utility in prospective drug design.

## The Drug Discovery Pipeline Impact

Traditional structure-based drug design:
- Protein crystallization: 6–18 months
- X-ray structure determination: 2–4 weeks
- Docking campaign: 1–2 weeks

With AlphaFold 3:
- Structure + docking: **12 seconds per candidate**
- Virtual screens of millions of molecules: feasible in hours

The Wellcome Trust-funded Structural Genomics Consortium has already screened 4.2 million compounds against 12 previously "undruggable" targets using the new tool.
