## The Cosmic Web

Cosmologists have long predicted that matter in the universe isn't distributed uniformly — it forms a vast **cosmic web**: sheets and filaments of dark matter connecting galaxy clusters, with enormous voids in between.

Until now, direct imaging of these filaments has been beyond reach.

## What JWST Observed

Pointing at the galaxy supercluster **Abell 2744**, a team from the European Southern Observatory used JWST's NIRCam instrument to detect subtle distortions in the shapes of over 50,000 background galaxies — a phenomenon called **weak gravitational lensing**.

By reconstructing the mass distribution needed to produce those distortions, they produced a 2D dark matter map with unprecedented 0.3 Mpc resolution.

## Key Findings

- Filament widths match Lambda-CDM predictions to within 8%
- Three previously unknown dark matter halos detected connecting two major clusters
- The void regions are even emptier than models predicted

## What This Means

This is the most direct confirmation yet that dark matter forms extended structures, ruling out several alternative gravity theories (like certain variants of MOND) that predict more diffuse distributions.

> "We are literally seeing the skeleton of the universe for the first time. It's exactly where the models said it would be." — Dr. Yuki Tanaka, ESO

## Next Steps

The team plans to extend the survey to 12 additional superclusters over the next two observing cycles.
