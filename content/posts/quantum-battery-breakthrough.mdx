## What Is a Quantum Battery?

A **quantum battery** is a theoretical energy storage device that exploits quantum mechanical phenomena — superposition, entanglement, and coherence — to charge and discharge energy faster than any classical device.

The concept was proposed in 2012, but experimental demonstrations have been limited to cryogenic temperatures and tiny scales.

## The Breakthrough

The Adelaide team engineered a thin-film molecular quantum battery using **organic semiconductor chromophores** — molecules that maintain quantum coherence at 300 K (room temperature) for several hundred femtoseconds.

This is enough time to exploit **quantum speedup in charging**, a phenomenon where entangling N battery cells reduces charging time from proportional to N to proportional to log(N).

## Results

| Parameter | Classical baseline | Quantum battery |
|---|---|---|
| Charging time | 840 ns | 4.2 ns |
| Energy density | Equal | Equal |
| Temperature | 300 K | 300 K |
| Cycle stability | — | >10,000 cycles |

The 200x speedup matches theoretical predictions within experimental error.

## Path to Application

The researchers caution that scaling to practical capacities (Wh range) requires solving decoherence at larger scales. But the room-temperature result removes the single biggest barrier to real-world quantum batteries.

A startup spun out of the university, **Quanta Energy**, is already in discussions with EV manufacturers.
