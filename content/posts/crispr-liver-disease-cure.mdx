## The Disease

Transthyretin amyloidosis (ATTR) is a progressive, fatal disease caused by misfolded transthyretin (TTR) proteins produced by the liver. These proteins form amyloid deposits in the heart and nerves, leading to heart failure and neuropathy within years of diagnosis.

## The Treatment

Intellia Therapeutics and Regeneron developed **NTLA-2001**, a lipid nanoparticle-delivered CRISPR base editor that targets and permanently silences the TTR gene in liver cells with a single intravenous infusion.

## Trial Results

In the 54-patient Phase II trial:

| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Reduction in serum TTR | 94.3% |
| Patients achieving >80% reduction | 96% |
| Duration of effect (follow-up) | 24 months, sustained |
| Serious adverse events | 2 (unrelated to treatment) |

## Expert Commentary

Cardiologists not involved in the study called the results "transformative." Unlike current RNA-interference therapies which require quarterly injections, NTLA-2001 appears to deliver permanent gene silencing after one dose.

## Regulatory Path

Intellia plans to submit a Biologics License Application (BLA) to the FDA by Q4 2026. If approved, it would be the first CRISPR therapy approved for a systemic disease.
